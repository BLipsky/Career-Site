Overview

Civil engineering is a multifaceted discipline focused on the design, construction, and maintenance of essential infrastructure. Typical responsibilities in this field encompass various aspects of project lifecycle management. Civil engineers conceptualize and plan projects, utilizing computer-aided design software to create detailed plans and drawings. They oversee construction activities, ensuring adherence to specifications, codes, and regulations, while also managing budgets, schedules, and resources. Feasibility studies, environmental impact assessments, and risk evaluations are conducted to assess project viability. Beyond construction, civil engineers are engaged in ongoing maintenance and repair efforts to sustain infrastructure functionality and safety over time.

Education

Civil engineers typically earn a bachelor's degree in civil engineering or a related field, with coursework in math, physics, engineering mechanics, and construction systems. Employers often prefer graduates from ABET-accredited programs. Cooperative-education programs and internships offer practical experience. While licensure isn't necessary for entry-level roles, civil engineers usually pursue a Professional Engineer (PE) license for public service. PE licensure involves a bachelor's degree from an ABET-accredited program, passing the FE and PE exams, relevant work experience, and state-specific requirements. Certifications in specialized areas, like coastal or geotechnical engineering, can also enhance career prospects. Continuing education may be required to maintain licensure in some states.

Salary
The typical median average wage for civil engineers as of May 2023, a little under a year ago, stood at about the amount of $95,890. This figure pretty much represents the midpoint where half of the workers in the occupation earned more and half earned less. Those in the lowest 10 percent earned below $63,220, while the highest 10 percent earned over $150,640. Most civil engineers maintain full-time positions, with some extending beyond the typical 40-hour workweek. Those overseeing projects may dedicate additional hours to ensure designs meet specifications and projects progress according to deadlines. This demanding schedule underscores the commitment civil engineers have toward the successful completion of their projects and the fulfillment of their professional responsibilities.

Work Environment

Civil engineers work in a variety of locations and conditions, including indoors in office settings and outdoors at construction sites. Some construction jobs require setting up a temporary office, such as in a trailer, to work onsite.

Source
Sustainability

Employment of civil engineers is projected to grow 5 percent from 2022 to 2032, faster than the average for all occupations. About 21,200 openings for civil engineers are projected each year, on average, over the decade.

Source
Schedule

Most civil engineers work full time, and some work more than 40 hours per week. Engineers who direct projects sometimes work extra hours to ensure that designs meet requirements and that the projects are on track to meet deadlines.
